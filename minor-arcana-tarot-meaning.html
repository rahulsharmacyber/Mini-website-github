Minor Arcana Tarot Meaning: All 4 Suits and What the Cards Are Telling You

The 56 cards of the Minor Arcana are where the real texture of daily life lives — your emotions, ambitions, decisions, money worries, and moments of clarity.

Mini Govil

Vedic Astrologer · Tarot Reader · 10+ Years Experience

When most people think of tarot, they picture the dramatic imagery of the Major Arcana: The Fool stepping off a cliff, The Tower struck by lightning, or The World dancer in her laurel wreath. But the 56 cards of the Minor Arcana are where the real texture of daily life lives.

They are the cards that describe your arguments, your ambitions, your money worries, your heartbreaks, and your quiet moments of clarity.

Understanding minor arcana tarot meaning is not just a reading skill. It is a tool for self-growth. Once you can fluently read the four suits, you stop seeing tarot as mysterious and start seeing it as a language that reflects your inner and outer world back to you.

This guide covers everything: what the four suits mean, how the numbered pip cards work, what the court cards represent, and how to use the Rider Waite Minor Arcana as your foundation for intuitive reading.

What Is the Minor Arcana?

A standard tarot deck contains 78 cards divided into two halves. The Major Arcana holds 22 cards depicting major life themes and archetypal forces. The Minor Arcana holds the remaining 56 cards, organised into four suits of 14 cards each.

Each suit runs from Ace to Ten. These are called pip cards, followed by four court cards: the Page, Knight, Queen, and King. The pip cards describe situations and energies, while the court cards represent personality types, people in your life, or aspects of your own character.

The word “minor” can be misleading. These cards are not less important than the Major Arcana. They are simply more immediate, concerned with the chapters of everyday life rather than the overarching story. If the Major Arcana is the novel, the Minor Arcana is every scene, conversation, and decision within it.

The Four Suits of the Minor Arcana

The four suits — Cups, Wands, Swords, and Pentacles — each correspond to one of the four classical elements, a domain of life, and a way of experiencing the world.

Learning the Cups, Wands, Swords, and Pentacles meaning is the single most important step in building a strong tarot foundation.

Cups: The Suit of Water and Emotion

  • Element: Water
  • Domain: Emotions, relationships, intuition, the subconscious, and creativity

Cups are the heart of the tarot deck. When Cups cards appear, the reading is pointing toward your emotional landscape: how you feel about a situation, what your intuition is telling you, the state of your relationships, and the deep currents running beneath the surface of your life.

Cups cards can be tender and romantic, like the Two of Cups showing a new partnership, or they can be heavy with grief and illusion, like the Five of Cups mourning over spilled cups while ignoring what remains. Water flows, and so do Cups. They speak of things in motion: feelings that shift, relationships that evolve, and dreams that surface from the unconscious mind.

In the Rider Waite Minor Arcana, Cups imagery is rich with water, chalices, and lunar light. When you see these cards frequently in a reading, the message is clear: the emotional dimension of your situation matters most right now.

Wands: The Suit of Fire and Ambition

  • Element: Fire
  • Domain: Passion, career, creativity, drive, vision, and action

Where Cups ask you how you feel, Wands ask you what you want to create. This is the suit of fire, spark, momentum, and the will to move forward. Wands cards speak to your drive and your dreams: your career aspirations, your creative projects, your sense of purpose, and your direction.

The Ace of Wands is a bolt of creative energy, pure potential waiting to be channelled. The Three of Wands shows someone already seeing results from their vision. The Nine of Wands depicts someone battered but still standing, holding the line.

Wands teach endurance, initiative, and the courage to pursue your vision even under pressure. Many people in career transitions, entrepreneurial pursuits, or creative blocks find that Wands dominate their readings. The suit is asking: where is your fire going? Is it burning bright, or scattered?

Swords: The Suit of Air and the Mind

  • Element: Air
  • Domain: Thought, conflict, communication, truth, decisions, and mental clarity

Swords are the most misunderstood suit in the Minor Arcana. Because many Swords cards carry difficult imagery — stormy skies, pierced hearts, figures in distress — beginners sometimes dread them. But Swords represent the mind, and the mind can be both the sharpest instrument we have and the source of our greatest suffering.

This suit governs communication, decisions, conflict, and the search for truth. The Ace of Swords is mental clarity: a breakthrough thought that cuts through confusion. The Three of Swords shows heartbreak delivered through words or betrayal. The Eight of Swords depicts self-imposed mental imprisonment: the belief that you are stuck when freedom is closer than you think.

The Swords suit invites you to examine your thoughts. Are you thinking clearly, or are anxiety and self-doubt running the narrative? This suit appears prominently when there is a decision to be made, a truth to be spoken, or a mental pattern to be broken.

Pentacles: The Suit of Earth and the Material World

  • Element: Earth
  • Domain: Money, work, health, home, the body, practical matters, and long-term security

Pentacles bring tarot down to earth. This is the suit of the tangible world: your finances, your work, your health, your home, your physical body, and everything that requires patience and consistent effort to build. Pentacles are slow, steady, and deeply grounded.

The Ace of Pentacles offers a new financial or practical opportunity. The Four of Pentacles warns against hoarding or fear of loss. The Ten of Pentacles is one of the most fortunate cards in the deck, representing generational wealth, a stable home, and the culmination of long-term effort. The Five of Pentacles shows struggle and hardship, often financial or health-related.

When Pentacles appear in your reading, the question being asked is: how are you building, sustaining, and protecting what matters in the physical world? This suit rewards diligence and practical thinking.

Tarot Number Meanings: What Each Number Tells You

One of the fastest ways to read pip cards confidently is to understand tarot number meanings. Each number carries a universal energy that applies across all four suits.

  • Ace (1): Pure potential and new beginnings. The seed of the suit in its most undiluted form.
  • Two: Balance, duality, and early decisions. Two energies meeting or needing to be weighed.
  • Three: Growth, collaboration, and first results. Energy moving outward and beginning to manifest.
  • Four: Stability, rest, and consolidation. A pause to assess and establish foundations.
  • Five: Challenge, disruption, and conflict. The number of change that arrives through difficulty.
  • Six: Harmony, generosity, and movement after hardship. Often a card of exchange or transition.
  • Seven: Reflection, inner work, and reassessment. The number of introspection before a new direction.
  • Eight: Mastery, momentum, and movement. Things accelerating or requiring disciplined effort.
  • Nine: Near completion, endurance, and the final test before the end of a cycle.
  • Ten: Completion, fulfilment, and the end of one cycle before a new one begins.

Once you know that Five always signals disruption, for example, you can immediately sense that the Five of Cups is emotional loss, the Five of Swords is conflict or defeat, the Five of Wands is competition or chaos, and the Five of Pentacles is material hardship without needing to memorise each card individually.

Tarot Court Cards: People, Personalities, and Energy Stages

Court cards are the most nuanced part of the Minor Arcana and the part that confuses beginners most. Each suit has four court cards — Page, Knight, Queen, and King — and they can represent real people in your life, aspects of your own personality, or energy you need to embody.

  • Pages: Youthful, curious, beginner energy. They can show someone just starting out or a message arriving.
  • Knights: Action, movement, and sometimes impulsiveness. They show the energy of someone charging toward a goal.
  • Queens: Mastery, emotional depth, and intuitive command of their element.
  • Kings: Full authority, leadership, and the most mature expression of their suit’s qualities.

When a court card appears in a reading, ask: is this a person I know? Is this who I am being right now? Or is this who I need to become in order to navigate this situation?

A King of Pentacles in a career reading might be telling you to approach your finances with the disciplined mastery of someone fully in control. A Page of Cups might signal that someone vulnerable or emotionally open is about to enter your life.

Why the Rider Waite Minor Arcana Is the Best Starting Point

The Rider Waite Tarot, first published in 1909 and illustrated by Pamela Colman Smith, was revolutionary for one simple reason: every card, including all 56 pip cards, features a fully illustrated scene. Before this deck, many tarot decks simply showed the relevant number of suit symbols on pip cards with no narrative imagery.

Pamela Colman Smith’s illustrations gave every card a story. The Eight of Pentacles shows a craftsman at work, hammering away with patient dedication. You do not need to memorise a keyword to understand what this card is saying. The Ten of Cups shows a family beneath a rainbow of cups, arms raised in joy. The image speaks directly to the heart.

This is why learning the Rider Waite Minor Arcana first gives you the most durable intuitive foundation. The imagery becomes a mental library that you carry into every reading, even if you later work with other decks.

Using the Minor Arcana for Self-Growth and Daily Guidance

Tarot for self-growth is one of the most powerful applications of the Minor Arcana. These cards are not about predicting a fixed future. They are about revealing the patterns, energies, and choices that are shaping your life right now.

A daily one-card pull from the Minor Arcana is one of the simplest and most effective spiritual practices you can build. Pull a single card each morning and ask: what energy does this card want me to be aware of today?

A Three of Wands might invite you to look ahead with confidence. A Seven of Swords might ask you to examine where you or someone around you is not being fully honest.

When reading multiple cards, pay attention to which suits dominate. A spread full of Cups calls your attention to relationships and feelings. A spread dominated by Swords suggests that mental clarity or conflict resolution is the primary work ahead. Wands-heavy spreads point toward action and ambition, while Pentacles clusters speak to practical decisions and material concerns.

How the Four Suits Connect to Astrology

The four tarot suits align directly with the four classical elements, which in turn connect to the twelve zodiac signs in groups of three.

  • Cups (Water): Cancer, Scorpio, and Pisces — the most emotionally intuitive signs of the zodiac.
  • Wands (Fire): Aries, Leo, and Sagittarius — signs known for passion, leadership, and creative drive.
  • Swords (Air): Gemini, Libra, and Aquarius — signs associated with intellect, communication, and rational analysis.
  • Pentacles (Earth): Taurus, Virgo, and Capricorn — signs known for practicality, patience, and long-term vision.

Understanding these elemental links deepens your readings considerably, particularly when combining tarot with Vedic or Western astrology.

A Quick Reference to the Minor Arcana Suits Explained

  • Cups: Water, emotions, relationships, the heart, intuition. Ask: how do I feel about this?
  • Wands: Fire, ambition, creativity, career, passion. Ask: what do I want to build or pursue?
  • Swords: Air, thought, conflict, truth, decisions. Ask: what am I thinking, and is it helping me?
  • Pentacles: Earth, money, health, home, work. Ask: what needs to be built, sustained, or protected in the material world?

Final Thoughts: The Minor Arcana Is Where Life Actually Happens

The Major Arcana will tell you that you are going through a period of profound transformation. The Minor Arcana will tell you exactly which Tuesday that transformation shows up in your inbox, in your heart, and in your bank account.

When you understand minor arcana tarot meaning — the suits, the numbers, and the court cards — you gain a complete map of human experience. You can trace the arc of any situation from the spark of potential in the Ace all the way through to the completion of the Ten.

You can identify the emotional, mental, practical, and energetic dimensions of whatever you are navigating. The cards are not telling you what will happen. They are reflecting back the energies already in motion and inviting you to make conscious choices within them.

That is the real gift of the Minor Arcana, and it is why these 56 cards deserve just as much reverence and study as the dramatic archetypes of the Major.
